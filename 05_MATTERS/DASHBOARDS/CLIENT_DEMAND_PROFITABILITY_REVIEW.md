# Client Demand Profitability Review

_Governed by the system subject to ML1 override._

## Purpose

The matter list must show real client demand, not only open files and task
labels. A firm can look controlled while still being unprofitable if the work
mix creates too much ML1 time, weak collection, low-margin customization, or
stale follow-up drag.

This review answers:

1. Which client work is actually live?
2. Which live work is profitable or potentially profitable?
3. Which work is consuming ML1 attention without enough revenue quality?
4. Which work should be priced, delegated, paused, collected, closed, or grown?

## Profitability Standard

Levine Law is genuinely profitable only if the live matter mix satisfies all
of the following:

| Standard | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Revenue quality | Work is priced, scoped, and collected in a way that supports margin |
| ML1 leverage | ML1 is doing judgment work, not avoidable admin or low-value drafting |
| Demand focus | The firm says yes to work that fits the target business, not every open loop |
| Throughput | Active matters move without repeated restart costs or unclear next actions |
| Repeatability | The best matters generate reusable playbooks, precedents, referrals, or retainers |
| Cash discipline | New work does not expand where retainers, AR, or pricing are unresolved |
| Non-ML1 capacity | Qualified work can be advanced by staff, contractor, clerk, freelancer, or system support before ML1 review |

## Required Matter Fields

Each active Essential, Strategic, and Standard matter should be reviewed using
these fields:

| Field | Required answer |
|---|---|
| Reality of client demand | Is this live, stale, speculative, waiting, closing, or internal? |
| Revenue / collectability | Is there paid work, AR, retainer, quoted scope, or collection risk? |
| ML1 load | Is this heavy legal judgment, review-only, admin, or delegable execution? |
| Delegation / system fit | Can clerk, freelancer, template, checklist, or productized workflow handle part of it? |
| Profitability posture | Grow, price up, delegate, collect / pause, or exit / close |
| Next commercial action | The next action that improves profit, not merely activity |

## Initial Profitability Hypotheses

These are working hypotheses only, pending ML1 completion of the intake table.

| Area | Hypothesis | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Stream / Andersen essential work | Potentially high-value demand, but must be separated into paid live work vs pipeline/speculative work | Prevents urgent labels from hiding non-billable business development load |
| Aspire | Strategic client but possible AR / collection drag | Strategic work is not profitable if unpaid or if scope keeps expanding without cash discipline |
| Standard corporate follow-ups | Some may be profitable quick-turn work; some may be stale attention debt | Requires triage into reprice, close, or convert to paid diagnostic |
| Internal / affiliated work | HillSide and LL Onboarding should not inflate client-demand counts | Profit analysis must distinguish firm-building work from paying client work |
| Delegation candidates | Standard repeatable corporate tasks may be best margin source if templated and reviewed efficiently | Profitability likely depends on moving ML1 out of first-draft/admin throughput |
| Capacity constraint | Marketing and selectivity require non-ML1 capacity | More qualified demand only becomes profit if intake, drafting, follow-up, admin, and first-pass execution are not all ML1-bound |

## Big Question

What is needed to make the firm genuinely profitable?

The likely answer is not simply "more clients." It is:

1. A live matter list that distinguishes paying demand from stale, speculative,
   internal, or collection-risk work.
2. A pricing floor and deposit rule for each work type.
3. A weekly AR / retainer gate before new work expands.
4. A delegation map for repeatable corporate, intake, admin, and first-draft
   work.
5. Non-ML1 capacity for intake, client follow-up, document production,
   first-pass drafting, checklist execution, collections support, and matter
   status maintenance.
6. A stop-doing list for work that is emotionally or strategically tempting but
   does not produce durable profit.
7. A target mix: fewer low-margin open loops, more paid diagnostics,
   scoped corporate packages, fractional counsel retainers, and high-value
   regulated/business-structure work.
8. A weekly review that asks, for every live matter: does the next action move
   cash, margin, client value, or leverage?

## Non-ML1 Capacity Requirement

More marketing and more selectivity require capacity outside ML1. Otherwise
qualified demand still bottlenecks at the same place.

| Capacity layer | Needed for | Profit role |
|---|---|---|
| Intake / qualification support | Sorting inquiries, collecting facts, confirming budget, routing to paid diagnostic | Prevents weak-fit leads from consuming ML1 judgment time |
| Matter admin support | Retainers, signatures, reminders, document collection, file closing, AR follow-up | Converts demand into collected revenue and closes open loops |
| Legal production support | First-pass drafting, precedent assembly, closing checklists, corporate updates | Lets ML1 review and decide rather than originate every artifact |
| Marketing execution support | Publishing, repurposing, email follow-up, funnel maintenance, CRM hygiene | Keeps qualified demand generation running without ML1 content/admin drag |
| Finance / collections support | AR tracking, retainer refresh, payment gating, profitability snapshots | Stops strategic work from expanding while cash discipline is unresolved |

The capacity principle is:

> ML1 should own judgment, client strategy, legal risk, pricing authority, and
> final approval. Non-ML1 capacity should own preparation, production,
> follow-up, administration, and operating cadence.

## Marketing / Selectivity / Capacity Loop

| Lever | Without non-ML1 capacity | With non-ML1 capacity |
|---|---|---|
| More marketing | More inbound noise and more ML1 triage | More qualified opportunities and better choice set |
| More selectivity | Better standards, but slow enforcement | Fast declines, paid diagnostics, clean scope gates |
| Better pricing | Quotes still require ML1 bottleneck | Repeatable packages and deposit rules can be administered |
| Delegation | The idea exists but work still sits with ML1 | Repeatable work moves through staff/freelancer/system lanes |
| Profitability | Revenue depends on ML1 stamina | Revenue depends on controlled throughput and margin |

## Two Profitability Hypotheses

The current strategic fork is not simply "do more marketing." There are two
different profitability hypotheses with different capacity assumptions.

| Hypothesis | Growth motion | Capacity sold | Core bet | Main risk | What must be true |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| H1 — Funnel 3 / ML1 capacity | Crank up Funnel 3 authority marketing for fintech / regulated business work | ML1 judgment, advisory, and strategic counsel time | Highest-margin path is selling scarce ML1 expertise into better-fit matters | Bottlenecks at ML1; creates premium demand but limited throughput | Funnel 3 demand is high-value, collectible, selective, and priced high enough to justify ML1 time |
| H2 — Funnel 2 / external fee earner | Crank up ads for Funnel 2 Corporate Health Check / diagnostic-style offers | External fee earner production time, with ML1 review and governance | Scalable path is paid diagnostics and scoped corporate work delivered with leverage | Ads create low-quality demand or supervision burden exceeds margin | Acquisition cost, external fee earner cost, ML1 review time, and conversion rate still leave healthy contribution margin |

## Hypothesis Test Design

| Test area | H1 — Funnel 3 / ML1 capacity | H2 — Funnel 2 / external fee earner |
|---|---|---|
| Demand source | Authority site, referral, conference/thought leadership, niche credibility | Paid ads, landing page, lead magnet, paid diagnostic, retargeting |
| Offer | Premium advisory / fractional counsel / regulated business counsel | Corporate Health Check / scoped diagnostic / structured corporate package |
| Unit economics | ML1 effective hourly or fixed-fee margin after admin time | Contribution margin after ad spend, external fee earner cost, and ML1 review |
| Capacity bottleneck | ML1 calendar and judgment bandwidth | Intake quality, fee earner execution quality, ML1 review load |
| Selectivity gate | High-value client, budget, strategic fit, urgency, retainer | Qualification call, paid diagnostic, deposit, clear exclusions |
| Success signal | Fewer but larger matters with clean payment and high strategic value | Repeatable paid diagnostics converting into profitable downstream work |
| Failure signal | More ML1 demand without enough price lift or capacity relief | Leads are cheap but low-fit, external work needs too much ML1 repair, or conversion is weak |

## Decision Frame

These hypotheses can both be valid, but they should not be confused:

- H1 is a premium expert-capacity model.
- H2 is a leveraged production / diagnostic model.

The firm should measure them separately. The key question is which path creates
more durable profit per unit of ML1 attention.
