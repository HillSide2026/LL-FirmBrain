# Andersen Lane — Enterprise Qualification Criteria

## Purpose

This document defines what "qualified" means for a prospective end-client file
in either Andersen lane matter (trade remedies or market access).

A track moves from `contacted` to `qualified` only when all threshold criteria
are met. Partial satisfaction does not qualify a track — it identifies what
still needs to be resolved before qualification.

These criteria apply to both lanes. Lane-specific notes are added where the
standard differs.

---

## Threshold Criteria — All Must Be Met

### 1. End Client Identity Confirmed

A real, identified organization is behind the file. An industry group, sector
association, or unnamed "prospective client" does not satisfy this criterion.

- The organization's name is known.
- The organization has standing or a direct interest in the specific issue
  (i.e., they are an affected domestic producer, importer, or market
  participant — not a bystander).

### 2. Legal Issue Is Concrete

The file has a defined legal issue, not a general commercial interest or
sector complaint.

- **Trade remedies:** a specific CITT proceeding is identified or a specific
  trade remedy instrument is in scope (anti-dumping, countervailing duties,
  safeguards); the client's position in the proceeding is clear (complainant,
  respondent, interested party, importer seeking exclusion, etc.)
- **Market access:** a specific barrier is identified (tariff classification,
  quota allocation, SPS measure, regulatory approval, export permit, etc.) and
  a specific country or route is in scope.

### 3. Scope of Work Is Definable

We can describe, in a sentence or two, what LL would actually be hired to do.
This does not need to be a formal engagement letter scope — but it needs to
be specific enough to generate a preliminary fee estimate.

Examples that pass: "represent the client in CITT GC-2026-001 safeguard inquiry
proceedings including submissions and witness preparation"; "advise on CFIA
import requirements for pecan shipments from Israel and prepare import
documentation."

Examples that fail: "provide trade advice"; "help with market entry."

### 4. Decision-Maker Access Confirmed

Our contact is either:
- the decision-maker who can authorize retaining counsel, or
- has confirmed they have direct access to that person and will facilitate
  the decision.

A contact who is monitoring the situation but has no authority and no path to
authority does not satisfy this criterion.

### 5. Timing Is Real

There is a concrete reason why the file needs to move in a defined timeframe.

- **Trade remedies:** a CITT filing deadline, questionnaire response window,
  public hearing date, or expiry review timeline is the clearest signal.
- **Market access:** a shipment date, regulatory deadline, or business
  transaction timeline.

A general "we should deal with this eventually" does not satisfy this
criterion.

### 6. Fee Path Is Clear

The fee routing through Andersen to 17513721 Canada Inc is confirmed or
confirmable. Specifically:

- Andersen has confirmed (or it is reasonable to confirm with one conversation)
  that they will route this file through the Andersen-LL channel.
- There is no indication the end client intends to retain counsel directly
  outside the Andersen relationship.

### 7. No Apparent Conflict

A basic conflict check has been run and no issue is apparent. This does not
require a full formal check — it requires that ML1 has turned their mind to
the question and no obvious conflict has surfaced.

## Using These Criteria

- Apply these criteria when reviewing a `contacted` track for advancement.
- If a criterion is not met, record what is outstanding as the explicit next
  step for that track — do not leave it as a generic "confirm details."
- A track that has been `contacted` for more than 60 days without meeting
  all criteria should be reviewed for demotion to `inactive`.
