# F02 Industry Targeting Rule

**Project:** LL Planning
**Project ID:** HBP-008

## Rule

F02 Corporate Health Check should not be marketed as a broad corporate diagnostic
for all Ontario operators during the 2026-2027 survival period.

It should be focused on industries that reinforce one of three strategic lanes:

1. fintech / payments / financial-services experience;
2. trade-impacted operating industries;
3. Rousseau Mazzuca-referable construction operators.

This lets F02 remain useful without becoming a generic corporate funnel that
consumes ML1 time but does not build strategic advantage.

## Priority Industry Lanes

| Lane | Target operators | Strategic reason |
|---|---|---|
| Fintech-adjacent operators | fintech, payments, MSB, PSP, embedded finance, software or platform businesses with regulated payment flows | Reinforces F03 authority and can route into fintech retainer opportunities |
| Trade-impacted operators | lumber, steel, plastics, import/export-exposed manufacturers, distributors, and supply-chain operators | Connects corporate/contract work to trade-impact experience and higher-value operating risk |
| Construction / lien-risk operators | mid-market construction operators, suppliers, subcontractors, project-facing businesses, and operators with potential lien claims | Creates referable work for Rousseau Mazzuca while preserving LL's role in corporate/contracts diagnostics |

## F02 Qualification Preference

F02 should prefer operators that meet at least one industry-lane test plus the
normal fit tests:

- at least CAD 5,000,000 in annual cash flow;
- meaningful operating business;
- ability to pay for a diagnostic;
- recurring or downstream legal need;
- decision-maker access;
- not a low-value commodity request.

Operators below CAD 5,000,000 in annual cash flow should generally not receive
ML1-heavy Health Check attention. Where they have a real need but are below the
cash-flow gate, they should be routed toward monetizable information products
where possible.

The canonical information-product off-ramp is
`../HBP-012_INFO_PRODUCT/F02_INFO_PRODUCT_LADDER.md`.

## Routing Logic

| If the Health Check surfaces... | Route |
|---|---|
| fintech/payments/MSB/RPAA/AML issue | F03 / LLP-039 pathway if recurring bounded advisory is plausible |
| tariff, trade exposure, import/export contracting, supply-chain disruption | LL corporate/contracts/trade-impact diagnostic pathway |
| construction lien, payment dispute, holdback, or construction litigation issue | Refer or coordinate with Rousseau Mazzuca as appropriate |
| below CAD 5,000,000 annual cash flow but has educational/self-serve need | Route toward HBP-012 / monetizable information product pathway |
| ordinary corporate hygiene with no strategic-lane connection | Accept only if high-fit, high-value, and low burden; otherwise defer or decline |

## Information Product Off-Ramp

| Product | Use when... |
|---|---|
| Corporate Starter Kit | Below-threshold operator has general corporate, contracts, governance, NDA, contractor, invoice, or payment-terms need |
| Corporate Assessment | Fit is unclear and the prospect should be routed before ML1 attention is committed |
| Fintech White Paper | Fintech-adjacent prospect needs education on payments, MSB, RPAA, AML, or banking-partner readiness but is not yet an F03 buyer |
| Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit | Operator is exposed to lumber, steel, plastics, import/export, manufacturing, distribution, tariff, or supply-chain contract risk |
| Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit | Construction or project-facing operator has holdback, payment, lien-deadline, or project dispute signals |
| Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack | Construction operator needs practical contract-risk education on scope, extras, delay, payment, backcharges, or lien-preservation issues |

## Allocation Consequence

F02 remains capped at 10%-20% of non-client LL build-time unless the industry-
focused version proves upgrade triggers under `F02_F03_ALLOCATION_MODEL.md`.

Generic F02 promotion should not receive serious build-time during 2026-2027.
