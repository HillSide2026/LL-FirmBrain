## Ownership Boundary Note

  valuation purposes even though they sit inside ML1's personal wealth boundary

## Income Distinction

- Business income means cash actually paid from `Levine Professional
  Corporation` or `17513721 Canada Inc` to Matthew, including salary, bonus,
  dividends, and approved distributions.
- Investment income means interest, dividends, and realized gains generated by
  Matthew's invested capital; broader market appreciation sits in the same
  investment-return lane for wealth tracking.
- Business income determines refill capacity and living-cost coverage.
- Investment income grows the capital base but should not be mistaken for
  durable refill unless it is realized, recurring, and explicitly accepted in
  the planning model.

## Core Planning Thesis

1. Preserve the liquid base first.
2. Improve recurring net savings and refill capacity.
3. Deploy capital selectively only when the path to the long-term targets
   improves without damaging liquidity or optionality.

## Major Capital Decision Rules

- Major budgets are set against unrestricted liquid capital, not total
  consolidated net worth.
- Corporate cash sitting inside `17513721 Canada Inc` is not treated as personal
  unrestricted cash unless a deliberate distribution, transfer, or other release
  event is documented.
- Private-business equity and unrealized sale theses may support the long-term
  wealth path, but they do not count as deployable housing capital unless
  monetization timing is documented credibly.
- Housing budgets may not assume future business-income growth unless the
  resulting net savings are already visible in `HBP-002_CASH_FLOW`.
- Refill assumptions must distinguish between business income to Matthew and
  investment income on the base; only the former counts as durable refill unless
  the latter is realized, recurring, and explicitly approved for use.
- Primary housing decision is open. Toronto, Japan, and other options are all
  candidates. No sequencing assumption is made between them.
- Toronto and Japan decisions share one capital pool and may not reserve the
  same dollars twice.
- A Japan primary housing scenario is modeled as a standalone what-if below
  and does not depend on the Toronto decision resolving first.

## Housing Linkage

`TORONTO_HOUSING` and `JAPAN_HOME_DECISION` are downstream decision projects.

Neither project has sequencing priority over the other. The primary housing
decision is open across all options.
