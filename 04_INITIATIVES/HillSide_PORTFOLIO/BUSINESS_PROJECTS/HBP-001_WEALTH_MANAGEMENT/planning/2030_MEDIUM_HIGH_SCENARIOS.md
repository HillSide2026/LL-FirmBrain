# 2030 Medium And High Scenarios

Project ID: `HBP-001`
Stage: `Planning`

## Planning Use

This file records medium-case and high-case scenario thinking for ML1's 2030
wealth, business, housing, and lifestyle position.

These are **scenarios**, not bankable forecasts, approved budgets, investment
advice, tax advice, or execution instructions. They are intended to help ML1
compare coherent possible futures and identify the assumptions that would need
to become real before capital, staffing, housing, or lifestyle decisions could
be approved.

## Non-Bankable Scenario Caveat

The figures below are deliberately directional. They should not be used as:

- a bankable income forecast;
- a payroll or dividend plan;
- a tax-residency conclusion;
- a housing budget;
- a business valuation;
- a hiring authorization;
- a commitment to live in any location for any fixed number of days.

Before any scenario becomes decision-grade, the relevant assumptions must be
validated through accounting, tax, immigration/residency, legal, housing, and
business-operating review.

## ML1 Rate Ramp And Bundled-Time Model

The ML1 production line should not be read as a flat `CAD 480/hour` direct
Levine Law model.

### Baseline 2026 Production Formula

ML1 clarified the baseline logic for the original `CAD 240,000` collection
target:

```text
1,000 total ML1 capacity hours
x 50% realization
= 500 collected hours

500 collected hours
x CAD 480/hour
= CAD 240,000 collections
```

This means the `CAD 240,000` target is not just a revenue number. It embeds
three planning assumptions:

| Assumption | Baseline value |
|---|---:|
| Total ML1 annual capacity | `1,000` hours |
| Realization into collected hours | `50%` |
| Collected hours | `500` hours |
| Baseline hourly rate | `CAD 480/hour` |
| Baseline collections | `CAD 240,000` |

Rate-ramp and Andersen-bundle scenarios should therefore be tested against the
same underlying capacity question:

> How much collection can ML1 generate from the same or lower total capacity
> load if rate, realization, and Andersen / `17513721 Canada Inc` mix improve?

Important correction: if ML1 holds the same `500` collected-hour output and the
effective rate rises, total collections rise. Rate improvement should not be
modeled only as a way to preserve `CAD 240,000` with fewer hours. It can also
increase the ML1 production line materially while keeping total ML1 capacity
constant.

For scenario purposes, ML1's 2030 production should be treated as a bundle of:

1. direct Levine Law time; and
2. Andersen / `17513721 Canada Inc` lead-advisor time.

### ML1 Bundled Production Examples

The baseline `CAD 240,000` production line can be preserved with fewer
collected hours if rates rise. More importantly, if ML1 keeps the baseline
`500` collected-hour output, rate increases raise total collections.

#### Same Collected Hours, Higher Rate

| Effective collected rate | Collected hours | Approx collections |
|---:|---:|---:|
| `CAD 480/hour` | `500` | `CAD 240,000` |
| `CAD 600/hour` | `500` | `CAD 300,000` |
| `CAD 780/hour` | `500` | `CAD 390,000` |
| `CAD 850/hour` | `500` | `CAD 425,000` |
| `CAD 900/hour` | `500` | `CAD 450,000` |
| `CAD 1,010/hour` | `500` | `CAD 505,000` |

At the original `50%` realization assumption, `500` collected hours corresponds
to `1,000` total ML1 capacity hours. If realization improves, the same collected
hours require less total capacity; if realization worsens, the rate increase is
partly consumed by uncollected effort.

#### Bundled Mix Examples

Illustrative examples:

| Mix | Direct LL collected hours | Andersen / 175 collected hours | Approx production at scenario rates |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Baseline direct LL | `500` hrs at `CAD 480/hr` | `0` hrs | `CAD 240,000` |
| Mostly direct LL specialist | `500` hrs at `CAD 750/hr` | `0` hrs | `CAD 375,000` |
| Mixed LL + Andersen | `300` hrs at `CAD 750/hr` | `200` hrs at `CAD 772-1,010/hr` | `CAD 379,000-427,000` |
| Andersen-heavy lead-advisor | `150` hrs at `CAD 750/hr` | `350` hrs at `CAD 772-1,010/hr` | `CAD 383,000-466,000` |
| Premium Andersen-heavy | `100` hrs at `CAD 750/hr` | `400` hrs at `CAD 900-1,010/hr` | `CAD 435,000-479,000` |

Planning implication: ML1 can either hold the same gross production line with
fewer collected hours, or keep the same collected-hour output and materially
increase collections. The stronger 2030 scenario should usually model the
second effect unless ML1 deliberately chooses to trade rate upside for lower
workload.

If realization remains `50%`, then `300` collected hours still implies about
`600` total ML1 capacity hours. If realization improves, the same collected
production requires less total ML1 capacity. If realization deteriorates, the
rate ramp can be partly wasted by non-collected effort.

The key constraint is not only rate. It is whether ML1 can preserve scope,
quality, supervision boundaries, relationship control, and entity-level
reconciliation.

## Andersen / 17513721 Canada Inc Rate Conversion Note

The scenarios should not treat Andersen-sourced work as economically identical
to direct Levine Law billing.

Structural input from ML1 and the `17513721 Canada Inc` corporate identity
record:

- `17513721 Canada Inc` is a collaborating firm to Andersen Consulting.
- Under the Andersen Service Line, `17513721 Canada Inc` provides services to
  clients of Andersen Consulting and Andersen Consulting pays `17513721 Canada
  Inc`.
- Operational matters tied to the Andersen Service Line are tracked inside the
  Levine Law operating picture.

Current Andersen rate input from ML1:

| Item | Value |
|---|---:|
| Andersen starting billing rate | `USD 650/hour` |
| Management fee | `10%` |
| Royalty fee | `2%` |

ML1 provided the following Andersen / `17513721 Canada Inc` lead-advisor rate
ramp:

| Year | Gross hourly rate | Net hourly rate after 12% fees |
|---|---:|---:|
| `2026` | `USD 650/hour` | `USD 572/hour` |
| `2027` | `USD 700/hour` | `USD 616/hour` |
| `2028` | `USD 750/hour` | `USD 660/hour` |
| `2029` | `USD 800/hour` | `USD 704/hour` |
| `2030` | `USD 850/hour` | `USD 748/hour` |

The net-rate column uses the additive shorthand:

```text
gross rate x (1 - 10% - 2%)
```

If the management fee and royalty fee are both applied against the gross
starting rate, the simple net-rate shorthand is:

```text
USD 650 x (1 - 10% - 2%) = USD 572/hour
```

If the fees are applied sequentially rather than additively, the shorthand is:

```text
USD 650 x 90% x 98% = USD 573.30/hour
```

The practical planning range is therefore approximately:

```text
USD 572-573/hour before foreign-exchange conversion
```

CAD-equivalent rate should be modeled as:

```text
USD 572-573 x USD/CAD exchange rate
```

This is materially higher than the current direct Levine Law standard and
specialist hourly-rate anchors of `CAD 480/hour` and `CAD 550/hour`, but the
Andersen lane still requires explicit treatment of:

- realization and collection risk;
- whether the fee deductions apply to gross billings or collected receipts;
- whether any additional referral, platform, tax, currency, or payment costs
  apply;
- whether the economics are credited to Levine Law as gross collections,
  net collections, margin contribution, or another operating-credit measure;
- the fact that Andersen fee receipts land in `17513721 Canada Inc`, while the
  operating credit is tracked in the Levine Law planning picture.

Until those items are locked, Andersen work should be modeled as a separate
rate-converted lane inside the scenarios rather than blended silently into
direct Levine Law billing.

### Junior-Leverage Conditions

Junior Andersen leverage should be treated as plausible only if:

- Andersen Consulting accepts the staffing structure;
- the work can be scoped, supervised, and quality-controlled;
- junior output does not create reputational or client-relationship risk;
- ML1 supervision time remains bounded;
- realization and collections remain strong;

  work;
- the operating-credit rule into Levine Law planning is explicit.

operators under ML1's strategic control.

- delivery systems, intake, admin, and matter management mature materially;

## Housing And Lifestyle Treatment

Both scenarios assume the three-base lifestyle remains possible only if it
passes the HBP-001 guardrails.

Key no-go triggers include:

- post-close liquidity reserve breach;
- unresolved Japan or Canada tax-residency exposure;
- Japanese inheritance-tax exposure large enough to make the plan misleading;
- Canadian accommodation or tropical-country costs that erase the intended
  savings and lifestyle benefit;
- travel burden that reduces ML1's ability to supervise Levine Law or preserve
  health;
- any housing decision that double-reserves capital already needed for another
  unresolved housing path.

## Decision Standard

These scenarios become useful only if they clarify what must be made real.

The medium case requires:

- one proven fee earner;
- possible junior Andersen fee-earner leverage if the collaborating-firm
  structure supports it;
- controlled ML1 production;
- positive retained profit;
- three-base lifestyle costs that remain within HBP-001 guardrails.

The high case requires:

- two proven fee earners;
- possible additional junior Andersen fee-earner leverage if staffing,
  supervision, realization, and entity-level reconciliation are validated;
- stronger demand and management systems;
- materially higher retained profit;
- evidence that Levine Law has enterprise value beyond ML1 personal labor;
- three-base lifestyle execution that does not destabilize Canadian
  professional operations or tax/residency posture.

Until those facts are validated, this file remains a scenario-planning artifact
only.
