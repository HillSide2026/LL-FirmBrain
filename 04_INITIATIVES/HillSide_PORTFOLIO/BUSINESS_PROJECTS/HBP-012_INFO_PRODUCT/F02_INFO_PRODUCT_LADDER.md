# F02 Operational Product Ladder

## Role

This ladder defines the monetizable operational products that can absorb
below-threshold F02 prospects without creating ML1-heavy service work.

The ladder is not a replacement for the F02 Corporate Health Check. It is the
off-ramp for prospects that have a real educational or self-serve need but do
not meet the F02 Health Check gate, especially the CAD 5,000,000 annual cash-
flow preference.

These products should not be built as generic content products. The strongest
versions are operational decision products, diagnostic systems, qualification
systems, intake-compression systems, and structured risk-routing tools.

## Clean Model

| Product | Lane | Price posture | Purpose |
|---|---|---:|---|
| Corporate Starter Kit | General below-threshold operators | CAD 197-CAD 497 | Basic contracts, governance, contractor, NDA, invoice/payment terms, compliance checklist |
| Corporate Assessment | Cross-F02 | Free-CAD 97 | Operational risk and structure assessment that classifies, prioritizes, routes, and recommends next steps |
| Fintech White Paper | Fintech-adjacent | CAD 197-CAD 497 | Authority infrastructure on real-time payment rails, stablecoins, funds-flow architecture, liability allocation, RPAA interaction, and safeguarding structure |
| Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit | Lumber, steel, plastics, manufacturing, distribution | CAD 297-CAD 697 | Trigger-based risk product showing where contract structure breaks under pricing, tariff, FX, and supply-chain volatility |
| Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit | Construction / Rousseau Mazzuca referral lane | CAD 197-CAD 497 | Time-sensitive operational triage for payment, holdback, lien-period, notice, and upstream-funds risk |
| Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack | Construction operators | CAD 297-CAD 697 | Risk identification and negotiation-prioritization system for subcontractors and suppliers signing recurring bad paper |

## Product Types

| Type | Products | Strategic job |
|---|---|---|
| Routing / diagnostic products | Corporate Assessment | Standardize intake, compress qualification, segment leads, frame risk, and route to Health Check, F03, referral, operational product, or decline path |
| Trigger-based industry risk products | Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit; Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit | Attach to economic anxiety, operational disruption, external events, deadlines, and loss-containment moments |
| Authority / strategic positioning products | Fintech White Paper | Build authority, partnership credibility, and market positioning rather than immediate cash conversion |
| Construction-specific operational products | Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack | Convert repeated contract-risk pain into flat-fee review, recurring contractor relationships, or eventual outside-counsel subscriptions |

## Product 1 Status

Product 1 already exists in HBP-012 as the Corporate Starter Kit / Founder
Contract Template Pack. It remains the anchor product for general
below-threshold operators.

The immediate product-development question is not whether to create Product 1.
It is whether the next product should be:

1. Corporate Assessment, because it improves intake standardization, lead
   segmentation, upsell pathways, and authority across F02, F03, referral, and
   operational-product paths;
2. Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack, because it maps directly to repeated
   Ontario construction pain and creates a path into flat-fee review, recurring
   contractor relationships, and outside-counsel subscription work;
3. Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit, because it creates deadline-sensitive
   routing, loss-containment framing, and Rousseau Mazzuca referral moments; or
4. Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit, because it creates a differentiated
   legal/procurement/operational consulting angle around contract failure under
   volatility.

## Routing Rules

| Prospect fact pattern | Default routing |
|---|---|
| Below CAD 5,000,000 annual cash flow; generic corporate need | Corporate Starter Kit |
| Unclear fit; needs screening before any ML1 attention | Corporate Assessment |
| Fintech, payments, MSB, RPAA, AML, or banking-partner question; low or unclear ability to buy F03 | Fintech White Paper |
| Lumber, steel, plastics, manufacturing, distribution, import/export, tariff, or supply-chain contract issue | Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit |
| Construction payment, holdback, lien deadline, subcontracting, or project dispute signal | Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit, with Rousseau Mazzuca referral path where appropriate |
| Construction operator with contract-drafting or risk-review need rather than immediate dispute | Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack |

## Product Architecture Notes

### Corporate Assessment

The Corporate Assessment is not primarily an information product. It is a
qualification, intake-compression, and risk-framing system.

Positioning:

> Operational risk and structure assessment.

The output should classify, prioritize, route, and recommend next steps.

Inputs should include:

- corporate structure;
- ownership;
- industry;
- disputes;
- counterparties;
- cross-border exposure;
- contractor / subcontractor structure;
- regulatory touchpoints.

Outputs should include:

- risk categories;
- likely legal exposure;
- suggested workstreams;
- escalation score;
- recommended engagement pathway.

Best monetization:

- free or low-cost entry;
- consultation conversion;
- recurring counsel conversion.

### Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit

The Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit should not be positioned as a trade-law
guide. The better pain framing is:

> Your contracts were written for a stable pricing environment.

The angle is:

> Here is where your contract structure breaks under volatility.

The kit should focus on:

- price-escalation clauses;
- force-majeure mismatch;
- supply interruption;
- tariff pass-through;
- FX exposure;
- allocation clauses;
- exclusivity and minimum-purchase obligations;
- termination rights;
- change-in-law provisions.

Best target buyers:

- manufacturers;
- distributors;
- procurement-heavy businesses;
- construction supply-chain players.

### Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit

This product should not be educational-first. It should be time-sensitive
operational triage.

The product is not selling construction-law knowledge. It is selling:

- loss containment;
- deadline clarity;
- leverage preservation.

The triage should revolve around:

- what happened;
- when materials were supplied;
- who has not paid;
- whether a proper invoice was issued;
- whether a notice of non-payment has been delivered;
- whether the lien period is already running;
- who upstream may still hold funds.

This product can become a routing engine, intake funnel, or later a lightweight
SaaS workflow.

### Fintech White Paper

The Fintech White Paper is not primarily a lead magnet. It is authority
infrastructure, partnership infrastructure, and market-positioning
infrastructure.

The audience is:

- fintech operators;
- compliance leads;
- PSPs;
- sponsor banks;
- investors;
- regulators;
- sophisticated counterparties.

The paper should avoid generic "what is a stablecoin" content. It should focus
on:

- operational architecture;
- liability allocation;
- safeguarding structure;
- settlement mechanics;
- overlay services;
- RPAA interaction;
- bank disintermediation risk;
- orchestration-layer economics;
- control layers;
- funds-flow logic;
- regulatory-perimeter mapping.

This positions LL above generic fintech counsel and below massive institutional
firms.

### Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack

The Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack should not be positioned as contract
review. It should be positioned as risk identification and negotiation
prioritization.

The strongest version includes:

- red-flag matrix;
- must-fix issues;
- commercially tolerable issues;
- payment-risk indicators;
- flow-down traps;
- insurance mismatch;
- indemnity exposure;
- schedule liability;
- backcharge exposure;
- pay-when-paid mechanics;
- lien-waiver traps.

This product can align with flat-fee review, recurring contractor
relationships, and eventual outside-counsel subscriptions.

## Priority Sequence

For 2026, HBP-012 should not attempt to build all products at once.

Recommended priority:

1. Maintain and launch Product 1: Corporate Starter Kit / Founder Contract
   Template Pack.
2. Build Corporate Assessment as the routing layer for F02/F03/referral/info
   product disposition.
3. Build Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack if construction referral and operator
   conversations confirm repeated contract-risk pain.
4. Build Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit if Rousseau Mazzuca referral
   flow or deadline-sensitive payment inquiries are present.
5. Build Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit if trade volatility produces repeated
   contract-structure questions from manufacturers, distributors, procurement-
   heavy businesses, or construction supply-chain players.
6. Treat Fintech White Paper as authority infrastructure for F03 rather than an
   immediate cash-flow product.
7. Defer the remaining lane-specific products until there is evidence of
   traffic, referral demand, or repeatable questions.

## Strategic Assessment

The strongest near-term products are probably:

1. Corporate Assessment;
2. Subcontractor Contract Risk Pack;
3. Construction Payment / Lien Triage Kit.

These products map directly to operational pain, create immediate engagement
pathways, and fit Ontario SME buying behavior.

The Fintech White Paper is strategically valuable, but primarily for signaling,
ecosystem positioning, and institutional credibility. It should not be treated
as an immediate cash-flow substitute for F03 retainers.

The Trade-Impacted Contract Risk Kit sits between legal, procurement, and
operational consulting. That may become a differentiated niche if developed
properly.

## Guardrails

- These are operational products and information products, not legal advice.
- Purchase must not imply a solicitor-client relationship.
- The products should not invite individualized legal answers unless the buyer
  is routed into a separate paid service.
- Construction lien and payment-dispute products must preserve a clear referral
  path to Rousseau Mazzuca where dispute counsel is appropriate.
- Fintech products must preserve the distinction between low-priced education
  and F03 bounded advisory / retainer work.
