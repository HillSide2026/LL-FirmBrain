# Associate Model

**Project:** Levine Law 2027
**Project ID:** HBP-008

## Model Confirmed

The 2027 associate layer is a **part-time contractor arrangement**, not a salaried
employment. This is the only structure that is economically viable at a CAD 160,000
associate billings target. A salaried associate at competitive market compensation
(CAD 90,000–110,000) would require the associate to produce CAD 280,000–320,000 in
billings to generate a positive contribution, which exceeds available headroom if
ML1 holds to CAD 240,000.

The arrangement works for someone who is **deliberately part-time** — supplementing
other income, building their own practice, returning from leave, or working across
multiple engagements. $64,000 from 500 total hours (~333 billable hours) is
competitive for that profile. It is not adequate as a sole income.

## Associate Profile

| Attribute | Requirement |
|---|---|
| Call year | 4–5 years (Ontario) |
| Practice depth | Corporate/commercial — capable of running files end to end |
| Specialty | Financial services experience required: payments, MSB, AML/FINTRAC, RPAA, fintech regulatory |
| Supervision requirement | Minimal — file management judgment, not execution under ML1 direction |
| Work structure | Part-time contractor; other income source or deliberate part-time arrangement |
| Availability | Reliable 8–10 hours per week; steady rather than burst-based |
| Temperament | Entrepreneurial; comfortable with variable income; self-managing |

**Why financial services experience is required:** The associate must be able to handle
F03 payments/MSB/regulatory matters, not only F01 and F02 corporate files. Without
this, ML1 retains every F03 file and the payments practice cannot scale beyond ML1's
personal capacity.

**Why 4–5 years call:** End-to-end file management with minimal supervision requires
developed judgment on scope, risk, and client communication. A junior associate (1–3
years call) needs material supervision; the model assumes that cost is low.

## Compensation Structure

| Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Contractor split | 40% of collected fees on delegated matters |
| Billing rate (associate work) | $480/hr (standard); $550/hr (regulatory) |
| Effective hourly rate at 40% split | $192/hr (standard); $220/hr (regulatory) |
| Expected billings (target) | CAD 160,000 (~333 billable hours) |
| Expected billings (floor) | CAD 100,000 (~208 billable hours) |
| Expected compensation (target) | CAD 64,000 |
| Expected compensation (floor) | CAD 40,000 |
| Total hours (inc. non-billable) at target | ~500 hours/year (~10 hours/week) |

No base salary. No benefits. No exclusivity obligation. Compensation is entirely
variable on collected fees.

**Note on the 40% split:** 40% is at the upper end of reasonable for this profile.
It is defensible for someone running files genuinely end to end. A step-up structure
(35% until collections reliability is established, then 40%) may be preferable at
negotiation to protect contribution margin in the ramp period.

## Sourcing Strategy

**Primary method:** Referral through professional networks. This profile does not
respond to a job posting.

**Target candidate sources:**
- Former colleagues at boutique firms with banking or fintech practices
- Alumni of firms with established financial services regulatory groups
- Lawyers in an in-house role at a payments operator or MSB who want lateral income
- Lawyers returning from parental or family leave who want flexible re-entry

**Timing:** Sourcing must begin in H2 2026, before the LLP-033 activation gate is
formally met. The right candidate cannot be found in two weeks. Starting after the gate
is triggered means the associate is not active until H2 2027 at the earliest.

**Sourcing owner:** ML1.

## Retention Framework

The part-time contractor has low switching costs by design. Retention requires active
relationship management, not just competitive compensation.

| Retention driver | How Levine Law delivers it |
|---|---|
| Steady file flow | 8–10 hours/week of delegable work; no feast-or-famine cycles |
| Quality files | Substantive corporate/commercial and payments matters; not admin or formatting |
| Autonomy | Associate runs files within defined guardrails; not waiting for instruction |
| Clear scope | Matter eligibility list and supervision model defined before engagement |
| Growth pathway | If the model scales, associate is the first option for more hours and a more formal role |

---

## Matter Eligibility

The matter eligibility list is governed by LLP-033 and requires separate ML1 approval
before the first files are delegated. Working direction:

**Delegable (expected):**
- Standard corporate maintenance: annual resolutions, officer/director changes,
  registered address updates
- Bounded contract drafting and review on standard templates
- F02 remediation work arising from Health Check findings (defined scope)
- MSB registration and basic FINTRAC compliance work (subject to associate's
  financial services experience confirmation)
- RPAA registration and readiness assessments

**Not delegable without specific ML1 approval:**
- Bespoke commercial transactions
- Shareholder disputes or litigation-adjacent matters
- Novel regulatory questions or multi-regulator exposure
- Any file where ML1 has an active strategic relationship with the client

---

## Activation Gate

Per LLP-033, associate activation requires:

1. 3 consecutive months at or above CAD 20,000 in ML1 monthly billings
2. ML1 approval of matter eligibility list, supervision model, and compensation
   structure
3. Associate profile confirmed against the requirements above

Activation authorization is a separate ML1 decision, not automatic on gate satisfaction.
