# Assumptions and Constraints

**Project:** Ontario Corp / Locarno
**Project ID:** HBP-015
**Stage:** Planning

## Assumptions

- FINTRAC registration is active; verification will confirm current standing with no material deficiencies
- RPAA registration travels with the entity on a share sale; acquisition-of-control rules are manageable
- The Ontario corporation's no-Canadian-director requirement makes it more portable than 174 for certain buyer profiles
- ConnectPay can be approached for a direct, pre-revenue contracting conversation
- Rhizome (via HBP-010) is sufficient for Track B compliance workflow unless ConnectPay or a bank requires Sumsub
- A buyer for Track B or C exists and can be reached through the right channel once the package is assembled
- Track A and Track B readiness work can be pursued in parallel without conflict
- Proceeds from this sale are compatible with the Q4 2026 Matthew Capital / Kendal Securities restructuring timeline

## Constraints

- No active sale outreach is authorized during planning
- No meaningful funds-flow volume may be run through the entity without separate ML1 approval
- Software and institutional relationships must remain subordinate to the entity sale thesis — not become independent products or businesses
- All three tracks remain open; no track is foreclosed without ML1 decision
- HBP-013 (174/Granville) runs in parallel; ML1 time is the shared constraint
- ConnectPay contracting must preserve assignability, change-of-control treatment, and termination flexibility — no terms that impair sale

## Locked Planning Direction

- The project governs the entity sale and its supporting build
- The entity is not being built into an operating BaaS or payments business
- Track A is always-ready; Track B is the primary target; Track C is assessed as the build progresses
- FINTRAC standing verification is the first gate — everything else depends on it
