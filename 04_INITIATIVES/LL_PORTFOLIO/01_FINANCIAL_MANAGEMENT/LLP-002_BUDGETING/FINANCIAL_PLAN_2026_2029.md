---

## Relationship to Firm Strategy

This plan operationalizes `LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY`. It does not choose the
strategy; it tests whether the strategy is affordable, collectible,
capacity-safe, and capable of producing firm value.

The firm strategy sets the direction: move Levine Law from reactive,
transaction-driven intake toward a controlled Tier 5 payments / fintech
boutique with stronger recurring counsel, better matters, and less
founder-dependent economics.

Financial planning translates that direction into control gates:

- collected revenue;
- committed MRR;
- operating reserve;
- payment timing and collection risk;
- matter quality;
- capacity load;
- owner compensation;
- reinvestment capacity.

The strategic question is whether an activity moves Levine Law up the Tier 8 to
Tier 5 ladder. The financial-planning question is whether that movement improves
cash, margin, durability, capacity, and enterprise value.

Financial planning may therefore constrain strategy execution. A market,
marketing, staffing, or product decision that looks strategically attractive
still requires a financial path to better work, better economics, or stronger
referral access.

## Strategic Context

Two practice area bets drive the financial model:

**Bet 1 — Fintech/payments/CARF regulatory boutique (tier 5)**
Target: recognized Canadian boutique for RPAA, PCMLTFA/FINTRAC, and CARF compliance.
Client profile: Canadian fintechs, PSPs, crypto-asset service providers (CASPs), and
foreign operators with Canadian end-user exposure. Revenue model: retainer-anchored
with project work on top. Content engine: fintechlawyer.ca.

**Bet 2 — Corporate governance, $5M-$20M Ontario businesses**
Target: structured Corporate Health Check product for businesses in the Stage 3-4
growth band. Revenue model: fixed-fee diagnostic converting to remediation and
ongoing corporate work.

## Scenario Definitions

Three scenarios are modeled. All are without leverage (solo practice throughout).

**Best case:** Both bets execute. fintechlawyer.ca generates systematic inbound by Q1 2027. Retainer ramp hits 2-3 mandates by 2028. CARF wave produces multiple engagements 2027-2028. Corporate Health Check converting at the $5M-$20M band. Retainer escalation applied at each renewal.

**Base case:** Plan executes but without the acceleration. Content converts slowly. One retainer established by end of 2026; second by mid-2027. CARF generates scoping work but not a full implementation wave. Health Check inconsistent. Practice grows steadily but remains partially referral-dependent.

**Downside:** Q1 2026 pattern (uneven, insufficient conversion) persists into H2. fintechlawyer.ca does not generate systematic inbound. CARF timeline slips. Stream/SnowCap stays at current level but does not grow. No second retainer established before 2028. Practice remains referral-dependent and below capacity.

## Revenue Structure Target

The model should shift from project-dependent to retainer-anchored over the planning horizon.

| Year | Target retainer % of revenue | Target project % of revenue |
|---|---:|---:|
| 2026 | 30-40% | 60-70% |
| 2027 | 40-50% | 50-60% |
| 2028 | 50-60% | 40-50% |
| 2029 | 55-65% | 35-45% |

A retainer base covering 55-65% of revenue by 2029 means project work is upside,
not baseline. This is the structural shift that makes the practice resilient.

## Billable Hours Model

Solo capacity ceiling: approximately 1,000-1,200 billable hours per year.
Retainer hours and project hours compete for the same calendar.

| Retainer mix (2029) | Retainer hrs/yr | Project hrs available | Total hrs | Revenue at mid-rate |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 3 x Tier 2 | 936 | ~150 | ~1,086 | ~$726K |
| 2 x Tier 2 + 400 project hrs | 624 | 400 | ~1,024 | ~$744K |
| 2 x Tier 2 + 1 x Tier 3 | 1,144 | ~50 | ~1,194 | ~$687K |

Revenue beyond $750K before 2030 requires leverage (associate, articling student,
or technology-enabled delivery that shifts the hours-to-output ratio).
