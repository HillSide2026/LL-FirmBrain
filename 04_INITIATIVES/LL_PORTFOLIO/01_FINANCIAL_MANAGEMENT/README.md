# 01_FINANCIAL_MANAGEMENT — Financial Management

## Purpose

Govern Levine Law financial management as a single top-level program while
preserving distinct internal layers for accounting, management accounting,
budgeting, financial risk, cash/collections, and finance.

## Scope

Financial management includes:

1. **Bookkeeping** — what was booked, billed, collected, reconciled, paid, or
   held in trust.
2. **Management accounting** — internal analysis of how accounting facts behave
   as a law-firm business system.
3. **Budgeting** — approved baseline, targets, scenarios, and budget-control
   models.
4. **Financial risk / cash / collections** — under-collection, uneven revenue
   conversion, WIP funding burden, AR exposure, liquidity stress, and
   cash-risk adjustment.
5. **Finance** — capital, liabilities, strategic banking, tax interfaces,
   major asset/liability decisions, investment/reserve policy, corporate
   finance, acquisition financing, and partner/shareholder economics.

## Canonical Packets

- `LLP-001/` — accounting operations and accounting fact layer.
- `LLP-002/` — approved baseline, budgeting, and scenario control.
- `LLP-044/` — finance principles, invariants, policies, and CFO
  advisory output.

## Supporting Folders

- `management_accounting/` — cross-packet management-accounting classifications
  and diagnostic lenses.
- `risk_cash_collections/` — operating financial-risk, cash, and collections
  artifacts.

## Critical Boundary

Financial management is one top-level LL program, but its layers must not be
collapsed:

- bookkeeping records are not strategy;
- management accounting is not bookkeeping;
- budgeting scenarios are not bankable forecasts;
- finance models are not approved decisions;
- trust funds are not operating cash;
- revenue is not economically equivalent unless timing, security, and
  collectability are considered.

## ML1 Authority Statement

ML1 is the sole authority for interpretation, approval, pricing, capital
decisions, budget-baseline changes, financing decisions, and any operational
decision derived from financial-management artifacts.

## Explicit Prohibitions

The System must not:

- treat bookkeeping records as strategy by implication;
- treat management-accounting classifications as accounting conclusions without
  ML1 review;
- treat budget scenarios as forecasts or commitments;
- set prices, approve discounts, or declare profitability;
- treat finance models as decisions;
- count trust balances as operating liquidity;
- auto-feed financial outputs into operations, sales, or matter handling.
