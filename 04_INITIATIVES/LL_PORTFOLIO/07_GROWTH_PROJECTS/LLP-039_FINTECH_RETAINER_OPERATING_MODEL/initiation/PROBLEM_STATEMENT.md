# Problem Statement

Levine Law's managing lawyer has limited billable capacity: approximately 20 billable hours per week. At the current nominal law-firm rate of CAD 480/hour, the firm's annual owner-billed capacity is approximately CAD 499,200 before realization and collections leakage.

The firm has identified a potentially superior model: fintech clients may buy a CAD 12,000/month recurring advisory retainer for payments, digital asset, FINTRAC, RPAA, banking, and regulatory-risk advisory support.

The economic challenge is that a CAD 12,000/month retainer only produces premium economics if scope is bounded and delivery is standardized. At CAD 480/hour, the retainer implies 25 hours/month/client. To reach an effective CAD 800/hour yield, the average delivery load must move toward 15 hours/month/client without degrading client value.

Without a governed operating model, the retainer may drift into:
- uncapped fractional general counsel service;
- embedded compliance function;
- client management or operations support;
- regulatory exam, incident, launch, or remediation work without separate fees;
- slow-payment working capital exposure; and
- concentration risk without renewal and offboarding discipline.

## Core Problem

LL needs to convert fintech regulatory expertise into a repeatable, bounded, high-value recurring advisory product while protecting owner capacity and preserving premium economics.
