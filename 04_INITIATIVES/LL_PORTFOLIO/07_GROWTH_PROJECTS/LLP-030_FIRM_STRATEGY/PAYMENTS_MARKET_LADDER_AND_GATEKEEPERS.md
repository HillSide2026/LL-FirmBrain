# Payments Market Ladder and Gatekeepers

This note belongs to `LLP-030_FIRM_STRATEGY`.

It records the market-positioning insight that Levine Law is not already a
Tier 5 payments / fintech / financial-services boutique. The firm is climbing
toward that position.

Related financial-machine note:
`MARKET_POSITION_AND_FINANCIAL_MACHINE.md`.

---

## 1. Tier Path

Levine Law's working tier path is:

| Year | Target market position |
|---|---|
| 2026 | Tier 8 |
| 2027 | Tier 7 |
| 2028 | Tier 6 |
| 2029 | Tier 5 |

Tier 5 remains the strategic destination: an emerging specialist boutique known
and referable for defined payments, fintech, AML, RPAA, MSB, and related
financial-services work.

The immediate task is not to claim Tier 5 status. It is to move deliberately
from Tier 8 toward Tier 7, then Tier 6, then Tier 5.

---

## 2. Exposure Is Not Arrival

The firm may encounter clients, matters, events, or opportunities that belong
to a higher tier before the firm itself has fully earned that tier.

Examples:

- Stream is more like a Tier 6 client that Levine Law reached early. It is
  useful evidence and a valuable bridge, but it is not proof that Levine Law is
  already a Tier 6 firm.
- Infonex is akin to a Tier 6-7 opportunity. It provides public authority,
  financial-services positioning, and a reusable credibility asset, but it is
  not proof of Tier 5 market status.

The strategic rule is:

> Do not confuse exposure to a higher tier with arrival at that tier.

---

## 3. Gatekeepers

As Levine Law moves closer to Tier 5, the gatekeepers become more numerous,
more serious, and harder to pass.

Early gatekeepers may include:

- newsletters
- conference organizers
- event platforms
- associations
- sponsorship channels
- industry media
- paid visibility platforms

Later and more consequential gatekeepers include:

- other lawyers
- larger firms
- referral counsel
- bank and compliance professionals
- investors
- accountants
- repeat deal participants
- operators already embedded in the payments and financial-services ecosystem

The most important gatekeepers on the path to Tier 5 will often be other
lawyers. They control referrals, credibility signals, informal market ranking,
and access to serious matters.

---

## 4. Opportunity Discipline

As the firm climbs, more opportunities will appear. Some will be useful. Many
will be distractions.

Potential distractions include:

- sponsorship packages
- panels
- speaking invitations
- association memberships
- paid directories
- industry databases
- newsletters
- one-off networking events
- advisory circles
- generic fintech or crypto visibility offers

These should not be evaluated by whether they are interesting. They should be
evaluated by whether they move Levine Law up the market ladder.

---

## 5. Working Filter

A market opportunity is strategically useful only if it helps Levine Law move
from its current tier toward the next one.

Use the following questions:

- Does this put Levine Law in front of payments, fintech, lending, AML, RPAA,
  MSB, stablecoin, or financial-services decision-makers?
- Does it create proof of specialist authority?
- Does it improve access to serious referral sources, especially lawyers and
  adjacent professional gatekeepers?
- Does it generate reusable content, relationships, or credibility assets?
- Does it reinforce Tier 7, Tier 6, or Tier 5 positioning rather than scatter
  the brand?
- Is the cost proportionate to Levine Law's current stage?
- Is this a real rung on the ladder, or expensive proximity to the ladder?

The goal is not maximum exposure. The goal is tier progression.
