# Lead Magnet Spec — Founder Agreements Checklist

## Strategic Purpose

Pre-sell the founder agreement template packages (IP1, IP2) by surfacing the
specific gaps in a founder team's legal foundation. The checklist makes each
gap concrete and felt, so founders arrive at the template purchase already
understanding what they need and why.

Target: Type 1 (digital start-up, typically founder-led) and any multi-founder
company regardless of type.

## Format

Checklist / scorecard. Three-option response per item: Yes / No / Not sure.
"Not sure" is as important as "No" — it surfaces the liability the founders
know they have not addressed.

## Framing

Working title: **"What Every Founder Agreement Needs — And What Most Are Missing"**

Subframe: "A checklist for Ontario companies with two or more founders."

## Structure — Five Pillars

---

### Pillar 1 — Equity and Ownership

- All founder equity is documented in writing and reflects current ownership
- Share certificates have been issued to each founder
- Option or equity grant agreements (if applicable) are executed
- No informal equity promises exist that are not captured in a document
- The cap table matches the corporate share register

---

### Pillar 2 — Vesting and Founder Retention

- Founder shares are subject to a vesting schedule
- The vesting schedule has a cliff period (typically 12 months)
- Vesting accelerates on defined trigger events (e.g., acquisition, termination without cause)
- What happens to unvested shares if a founder leaves is defined in writing
- Good leaver / bad leaver provisions are documented

---

### Pillar 3 — Intellectual Property

- All IP created by founders before incorporation has been assigned to the company
- All IP created by founders after incorporation is assigned to the company automatically
- IP assignment agreements are signed by every founder
- No founder retains personal rights to IP used in the business
- Contractor or advisor IP contributions are assigned to the company under written agreements

---

### Pillar 4 — Governance and Decision-Making

- A shareholder agreement is in place and signed by all founders
- The agreement defines which decisions require unanimous or supermajority approval
- Board composition and appointment rights are documented
- Founder roles and responsibilities are defined and documented
- Compensation expectations — salary, dividends, expenses — are recorded in writing

---

### Pillar 5 — Exit and Transfer

- Right of first refusal applies to all founder share transfers
- A drag-along provision allows a majority to compel a sale
- A tag-along (co-sale) provision protects minority founders in a sale
- A shotgun or buy-sell mechanism is in place for deadlock situations
- Death and permanent disability are addressed — what happens to a founder's shares

---

## Gate Mechanics

Option B recommended: deliver the checklist without a gate; capture email at
completion in exchange for a score summary and "what to do next" guidance. Lead
captured at peak motivation after founders have confronted their gaps.

## Bridge to Product

- 3+ items answered "No" or "Not sure": direct CTA to IP1 and IP2 template
  packages at `legal.levine-law.ca`
- Frame: "These templates are designed to address the gaps above. You get the
  document, the instructions, and the legal foundation — without a bespoke
  engagement fee."

## Placement

- Within digital start-up and founder-focused blog content
- On pages addressing shareholder agreements, founder equity, and vesting
- On or linked from the IP1 and IP2 product pages as a pre-purchase
  self-qualification tool

## Production Notes

- Format: interactive scored form preferred; PDF fallback acceptable
- Single universal checklist — no type variants needed (founder agreement
  issues are consistent across company types)
