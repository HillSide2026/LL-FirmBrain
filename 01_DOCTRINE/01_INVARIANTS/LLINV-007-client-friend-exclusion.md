# LLINV-007 — Client-Friend Exclusion

**Invariant ID:** LLINV-007
**Status:** APPROVED
**Authority:** ML1

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the structural incompatibility between the client relationship and the friendship relationship. These two categories carry mutually exclusive obligations. They cannot coexist.

---

## 2. The Rule

A client cannot be a friend. A friend cannot be a client. The categories are mutually exclusive during any active engagement and for a reasonable period after its close.

---

## 3. Invariants

1. A friend may not become a client while the friendship is active. The engagement cannot commence.
2. A client relationship may not become a friendship while the engagement is active. The social relationship must not cross into friendship during the professional engagement.
3. The exclusion is bidirectional. It applies regardless of which relationship came first.
4. The exclusion applies during the engagement and for a reasonable period after close. It does not apply permanently — a former client may shift to a personal relationship after genuine closure of the professional relationship and passage of sufficient time.
5. A friend's referral does not extend the friend's relationship category to the referred party. A referred contact is assessed on their own relationship with ML1, not the referring friend's.

---

## 4. Why the Boundary Is Structural

A client is owed competent, objective, commercial legal service governed by fee, retainer, and professional duty. A friend is owed unconditional loyalty and altruistic reciprocity. These obligations conflict directly:

- The lawyer cannot bill a friend-client properly without corroding the friendship.
- The lawyer cannot give a friend-client hard advice without the friendship distorting the delivery.
- The commercial engagement creates expectations that erode the friendship when professional duties require positions the friend does not want to hear.

Overlap does not merely create awkwardness. It corrupts both relationships structurally.
