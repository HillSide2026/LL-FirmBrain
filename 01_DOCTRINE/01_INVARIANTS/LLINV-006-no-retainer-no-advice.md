# LLINV-006 — No Retainer, No Advice

**Invariant ID:** LLINV-006
**Status:** APPROVED
**Authority:** ML1

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the structural boundary at which legal professional obligations attach. Legal advice is a professional act. It creates obligations and liability. Without a retainer or formal engagement, it cannot occur.

---

## 2. The Rule

No retainer. No engagement. No advice.

A retainer or formal engagement is the precondition for any legal advice, regulatory assessment, or legal professional work. Prior to that threshold, the contact is a lead. After it, the contact is a client.

---

## 3. Invariants

1. Legal advice may not be given to any person without a retainer or formal engagement in place.
2. Regulatory assessment, legal structuring recommendations, or characterization of a contact's legal exposure are advice. They are prohibited before the threshold.
3. A substantive consult conversation, however detailed, does not constitute a retainer or engagement. Length and depth of conversation do not move a lead into the client category.
4. Pre-engagement analysis is permitted: emotional (fit, relationship read), financial (ability to pay, retainer sizing), and general non-legal business analysis (understanding the operator's model or problem space). These are not advice.
5. Goodwill, rapport, and preliminary discussion do not create professional obligations. Only a retainer or engagement does.

---

## 4. Boundary with Related Doctrine

| Doctrine | Scope |
|---|---|
| LLINV-006 (this) | Hard rule: the threshold at which professional obligations attach — no retainer, no advice |
| LLPOL-002 (Purse First) | Operational rule: no work commences until the retainer is received and cleared |
| LLPRN-003 (Lead-Client Distinction) | Principle: leads are not clients; defines the full identity distinction and intake pipeline |
| LLPRN-006 (Retainer Precedence) | Principle: secure the retainer before extending advice, time, or goodwill |

LLINV-006 is the structural floor. LLPOL-002 is the operational gate. LLPRN-003 and LLPRN-006 are the governing principles that give the rule context.

---

## 5. Why Invariant, Not Principle

The lead-client identity distinction (LLPRN-003) is a principle — it describes a relationship category and how to navigate it. This invariant states the one non-negotiable structural rule within that principle: the threshold below which no legal professional act may occur. That rule admits no exception, no judgment call, and no override below ML1 level. It is invariant.
