# LLPRN-26 - Bounded Fintech Retainer Model

Title: Bounded Fintech Retainer Model

Statement:
The $12,000/month fintech retainer is viable only as a bounded strategic legal advisory model. It must not become an uncapped fractional legal department, embedded compliance function, or crisis-response subscription.

Rationale:
At a nominal rate of $480/hour, a $12,000 monthly retainer implies approximately 25 hours of legal capacity per month. That is a strong recurring-revenue unit if the work is paid in advance, scoped tightly, and concentrated on high-value fintech advisory work. It becomes economically dangerous if the client treats the arrangement as unlimited access, embedded compliance support, regulatory crisis coverage, or ongoing operational management.

The model works because it converts scarce owner capacity into predictable, collectible, recurring revenue. Three such retainers can cover most of the managing lawyer's available billable capacity. That strength creates its own risk: client concentration is temporarily accepted, so each retainer must be governed by strict capacity, scope, renewal, and offboarding discipline.

Application Notes:
1. Economic unit: each $12,000/month retainer should be treated as a 25-hour monthly capacity allocation unless separately agreed.
2. Boundary protection: work exceeding capacity, requiring accelerated timelines, or involving expanded business, regulatory, incident, launch, examination, enforcement, jurisdictional, tax, or securities scope must be separately scoped.
3. Relationship posture: the firm remains external legal counsel. It does not become management, compliance staff, product owner, or a fractional general counsel infinity pool.
4. Concentration management: because each retainer may exceed ordinary client-concentration targets during the early model, renewal cadence, pipeline coverage, cash reserve, and offboarding readiness must be actively managed.
