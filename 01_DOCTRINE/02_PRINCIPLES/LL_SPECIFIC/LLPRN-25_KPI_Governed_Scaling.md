# LLPRN-25 - KPI-Governed Scaling

Title: KPI-Governed Scaling

Statement:
Scale the firm only against operational metrics, not intuition. Growth amplifies the operating model that already exists; it does not cure unstable realization, margin, utilization, or collections.

Rationale:
A small law firm trying to scale must manage to measurable operating conditions. If the core metrics are unstable, adding clients, staff, or practice complexity usually increases operational inefficiency rather than fixing it. The firm should know whether it is improving because realization, collection rate, A/R days, recurring revenue percentage, utilization, concentration, gross margin, and write-offs are moving into healthy ranges.

Target operating state is not maximum revenue or maximum client count. The target is predictable realization, predictable gross margin, predictable utilization, and predictable collections velocity.

Application Notes:
1. Working targets: realization rate 85-95%, collection rate 90%+, A/R days under 45, recurring revenue 40-70%, utilization 70-80% productive time, no client over 15% of revenue, gross margin 40-50%, and write-offs under 5% of billings.
2. Hiring gate: do not treat hiring as the cure for workflow volatility until the firm can forecast demand, margin, and collection behavior with reasonable confidence.
3. Review cadence: the KPI set should be reviewed regularly as an operating dashboard for strategy, intake, pricing, staffing, and collections decisions.
