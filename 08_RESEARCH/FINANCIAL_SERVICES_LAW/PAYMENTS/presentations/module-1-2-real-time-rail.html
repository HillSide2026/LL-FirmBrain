The Real-Time Rail

Canada's RTR project is intended to create a true modern instant payment rail — with real-time clearing and settlement, continuous 24/7 operation, and broader participation by payment service providers and fintechs.

Launch status  ·  Not yet live as of June 2026 — Payments Canada targeting Q4 2026

1.2
1.2 — Feature 1

Real-Time Clearing and Settlement

ACSS — Batch Clearing (Today)

  • Transactions accumulated and cleared in cycles
  • Deferred settlement — finality occurs after the clearing cycle
  • Mitigation windows between initiation, clearing, and settlement allow intervention

RTR — Real-Time Settlement

  • Transactions processed individually as submitted
  • Status confirmation expected within seconds
  • Settlement finality is almost immediate — no deferred window
Key Takeaway

Real-time settlement reduces delay, but it also removes mitigation windows as a risk-management tool. Institutions can no longer rely on clearing cycles to resolve issues before funds move. The financial operations and Risk & Compliance consequences of this shift are addressed in later modules.

1.2 — Feature 2

24/7/365 Operation

RTR is designed to operate continuously — including nights, weekends, and holidays. That may sound like a technology change, but it is also an operating model change. Payments no longer follow banking hours; they follow customer expectations.

Liquidity Management

Institutions must maintain sufficient liquidity to support payment activity at all hours — not just during business-day settlement windows.

Fraud Monitoring

Fraud detection and transaction monitoring must operate continuously. A fraud event at 2:00 a.m. on a Sunday is as material as one on a Tuesday afternoon.

Operational Support

Staffing, escalation paths, and incident resolution capabilities must be available at all times — not reserved for business hours.

Incident Response

Outage response, participant notifications, and remediation processes must function continuously across all hours and calendar days.

Key Takeaway

The payment rail never closes — which means risk management cannot close either. For many institutions, 24/7 operational readiness may be the most significant participation requirement.

1.2 — Feature 3

ISO 20022 Messaging

RTR is expected to use ISO 20022 — a modern messaging standard that supports significantly richer payment information than legacy formats. Instead of simply transmitting funds, participants can exchange structured data about the transaction.

Data ISO 20022 Can Carry

Payer Identity

Structured payer details including name, account, and identifier fields.

Payee Identity

Structured payee details — enabling improved reconciliation and matching.

Transaction Purpose

Standardized purpose codes describing the nature of the payment.

Remittance Information

Invoice references, line-item detail, and structured remittance data.

Operational Benefits

Automation

Structured data enables automated payment matching and straight-through processing — reducing manual reconciliation effort.

Monitoring & Compliance

Richer transaction data supports more precise fraud detection, AML monitoring, and regulatory reporting.

Operational Efficiency

ISO 20022 data fields allow participants to build more efficient, more automated back-office workflows — but only if systems are built to consume the richer format.

1.2 — Feature 4

Request-to-Pay

RTR is expected to support request-to-pay functionality — allowing a business or individual to send a payment request that the recipient can respond to before funds are transferred.

Initiator (Payee)

Sends a structured payment request — with amount, purpose, and payment reference

→

Recipient (Payer)

Reviews the request and chooses a response

Approve Decline Schedule
→

Payment Rail

RTR executes the approved payment in real time — only after payer authorization

Use Cases

Invoice settlement, bill payments, and collections — with improved visibility and direct payer control over authorization. May reduce unauthorized payment fraud.

Key Takeaway

Request-to-pay creates new ways to initiate payments while preserving customer control over authorization — a meaningful shift from push-payment models.

1.2 — Feature 5

Facilitating Innovation via Access

RTR is intended to support broader participation — extending access beyond traditional deposit-taking institutions to payment service providers, fintechs, and other non-traditional participants. Greater access encourages innovation and competition.

Opportunities

Broader Competition

Non-bank payment service providers can access the rail directly — reducing dependence on bank intermediaries and opening new market positions.

Product Innovation

Access to real-time infrastructure enables fintechs and PSPs to build new payment products and customer experiences on top of the RTR rail.

Governance Questions

Operational & Cyber Risk

More participants means more entry points. Operational, cybersecurity, and third-party risk management standards must apply consistently across all participant types.

Non-Bank Oversight

How governance, resilience, compliance, and oversight expectations apply to non-deposit-taking participants is an important open question as RTR participation expands.

Key Takeaway

Open access does not mean reduced oversight. It means more participants operating under robust and consistent standards — and greater scrutiny of how those standards are enforced across a more diverse participant set.
